Section 03 — Industry Insight
HK Building Safety: Awareness & Proactive Maintenance
Cracks in the Foundation: HK's Urgent Building Safety Call
Uncover the critical data behind Hong Kong's building safety challenges and government action.

Hong Kong faces a pressing challenge with building safety, as evidenced by nearly 5,000 ageing buildings awaiting mandatory inspections and repairs, with notices expired for 2,700 of them [1]. A recent spate of falling concrete incidents, including four in less than a month in July 2023, highlights the critical need for enhanced public awareness and proactive maintenance [2]. While owners bear ultimate responsibility, public views remain mixed on financial costs and effective measures [3]. Proactive government campaigns are crucial to bridge this awareness gap and prevent structural risks.

  • Prioritise owner engagement:** Develop targeted campaigns to educate building owners on their legal and financial responsibilities for maintenance.
  • Streamline compliance:** Simplify processes and offer clear guidance for owners to comply with mandatory inspection schemes, leveraging digital platforms.
  • Incentivise early action:** Explore subsidy programs or tax incentives for timely maintenance, reducing the burden on owners of older buildings.
  • Enhance enforcement visibility:** Publicise successful enforcement actions and the consequences of non-compliance to deter negligence.

Sources: [1] Hong Kong Free Press, July 20, 2023; [2] South China Morning Post, July 5, 2023; [3] Development Bureau, Report on Public Consultation on Building Management and Maintenance, 2004.
