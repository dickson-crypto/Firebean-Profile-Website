Section 03 — Industry Insight
Citizen Co-creation: Boosting Trust & Policy Outcomes
Co-create with Citizens: Boost Trust, Better Policy
Discover how participatory governance strengthens public trust and policy effectiveness.

A recent study reveals that e-participation is utilized in approximately 75.97% of cases within a dataset of democratic innovations, highlighting a global trend towards digital citizen engagement in governance (Deliberative Democracy Journal). This surge in community co-creation and citizen participation reflects a growing recognition that involving the public in policy design leads to more effective and legitimate outcomes. When citizens actively contribute, governments gain valuable insights, foster shared ownership, and build crucial public trust. This collaborative approach moves beyond traditional consultations, embracing genuine partnership to shape policies that truly resonate with community needs.

Implications for HK government departments:

  • Enhance Digital Platforms:** Invest in user-friendly digital tools to facilitate broader and more inclusive e-participation.
  • Foster Genuine Dialogue:** Move beyond mere consultation to establish sustained co-creation mechanisms, ensuring citizen input directly influences policy.
  • Build Transparency:** Clearly communicate how citizen feedback is integrated into policy decisions to reinforce trust and accountability.
  • Targeted Engagement:** Develop strategies to engage diverse community segments, including underrepresented groups, to ensure equitable participation.

Source: Deliberative Democracy Journal; Open Government Partnership.
