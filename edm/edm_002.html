Modernizing Occupational Safety: The Tech-Driven Roving Exhibition
How the Civil Service Bureau used interactive technology to make OSH engaging.

When the Civil Service Bureau needed to promote occupational safety and health (OSH) through technology, Firebean designed a roving exhibition that broke away from traditional static displays. We integrated interactive tech demonstrations and hands-on experiences to show, rather than just tell, how technology elevates workplace safety. By transforming dry safety protocols into engaging, tangible experiences, we successfully captured public attention across multiple districts. This approach proves that complex government messages can be effectively communicated when paired with the right experiential design.

Key Outcomes:

  • Highly interactive tech-focused displays
  • Multi-district roving format for maximum reach
  • Increased public understanding of OSH innovations

Cross-Industry Synergy: What Government Can Learn from Brand Partnerships
How Yves Rocher and TeaDaye amplified their reach through strategic collaboration.

When skincare brand Yves Rocher partnered with premium beverage brand TeaDaye, Firebean orchestrated a cross-promotional campaign that leveraged both audiences. Through targeted PR consulting and influencer seeding, we created a unified narrative that drove mutual brand visibility.

Section 03 — Industry Insight
AI in HK Gov Communications 2026
HK Gov Embraces AI: Smarter Public Engagement
AI is revolutionizing how governments connect with citizens and gather feedback.

A recent McKinsey research from early 2026 reveals that nearly 70% of white-collar workers in Hong Kong now utilize AI, with over 90% engaging daily, signaling a significant shift in workplace technology adoption. This trend extends to public communications, where AI-powered tools are transforming citizen engagement and policy feedback mechanisms. Globally, 74% of public servants now use AI, with most adoption occurring in the past year. This surge is driven by the ability of AI to enhance strategic planning, stakeholder engagement, and operational efficiency. For Hong Kong government departments, this means:

  • Streamlined citizen services through advanced chatbots, improving accessibility and response times.
  • Enhanced policy development by leveraging sentiment analysis for real-time public feedback.
  • Greater efficiency in communication strategies, allowing for more targeted and effective outreach.
  • Opportunities for PR agencies to offer specialized AI-driven communication solutions and analytics.

Source: McKinsey Research (early 2026), Public Sector AI Adoption Index (2026)
