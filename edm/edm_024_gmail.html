Section 03 — Industry Insight
HK Gov Reputation & Crisis Management
Building Trust: Proactive Reputation for HK Gov
Navigating public perception: data-driven strategies for government credibility.

According to a May 2025 survey by the Hong Kong Institute of Asia-Pacific Studies at CUHK, only 33.2% of respondents expressed trust in the Central Government, with 28.9% distrusting and 33.9% neutral. This highlights the critical need for robust brand reputation management and crisis communications within the public sector. In today's hyper-connected digital landscape, public-sector crises unfold rapidly, with narratives spreading at algorithmic speed and trust proving increasingly fragile. Proactive reputation management is no longer a luxury but a necessity to safeguard departmental credibility and ensure effective governance.

The trend emphasizes a shift from reactive damage control to strategic, continuous engagement that builds and maintains public confidence. Government departments must recognize that every interaction, policy, and public statement contributes to their overall reputation.

  • Develop comprehensive, data-informed crisis communication plans with clear protocols for rapid response.
  • Foster transparency and open dialogue to address public concerns proactively, leveraging digital platforms effectively.
  • Invest in continuous monitoring of public sentiment and media narratives to identify potential issues before they escalate.
  • Cultivate consistent messaging across all channels to reinforce departmental values and commitment to public service.

Source: Hong Kong Institute of Asia-Pacific Studies, CUHK (May 2025)
