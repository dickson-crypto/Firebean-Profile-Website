Section 03 — Industry Insight
Youth Anti-Drug Campaigns: Experiential vs. Traditional
Beyond PSAs: Engaging HK Youth in Anti-Drug Efforts
Traditional anti-drug ads fall flat. Discover how experiential campaigns captivate Hong Kong's youth.

In the first quarter of 2025, Hong Kong reported 285 young drug abusers aged under 21, highlighting a persistent challenge in youth substance abuse prevention (info.gov.hk). Traditional public service announcements (PSAs) often struggle to resonate with this demographic, with research indicating limited influence on drug use perceptions and a potential 'boomerang effect' where unrealistic ads are associated with increased skepticism (researchgate.net). This trend underscores the urgent need for innovative, data-driven strategies that move beyond conventional messaging to foster genuine engagement and behavioral change.

Experiential campaigns, which immerse participants in interactive and memorable activities, are proving significantly more effective. These approaches build empathy, provide practical coping mechanisms, and create positive peer environments, directly addressing the social and psychological factors influencing youth drug use. For government departments, this shift means embracing creative, participatory methods that empower young people rather than simply lecturing them.

  • Reallocate resources from generic PSAs to interactive workshops and events.
  • Collaborate with youth organizations to co-create authentic, relatable prevention content.
  • Leverage digital platforms for engaging, gamified educational experiences.
  • Implement long-term, multi-faceted programs that integrate prevention into youth development initiatives.

Source: info.gov.hk, researchgate.net
