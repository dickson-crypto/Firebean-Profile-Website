Section 03 — Industry Insight
Inclusive Design: Broadening Gov Reach & Trust
Accessible Campaigns: Unlocking Public Trust in Hong Kong
Discover how inclusive design in government campaigns builds trust and expands reach.

In 2020, approximately 7.1% of Hong Kong's population, or 534,200 individuals, were identified as persons with disabilities (Census and Statistics Department, HKSAR, 2021). This significant demographic highlights the critical need for inclusive design and accessibility in government public campaigns. The trend towards greater inclusivity recognizes that accessible communication and event planning not only fulfill social responsibility but also broaden government reach and foster public trust by ensuring all citizens can participate fully.

  • Enhanced Public Engagement:** Accessible campaigns ensure that a larger segment of the population, including those with disabilities, can understand and interact with government initiatives, leading to more comprehensive public participation.
  • Increased Trust and Legitimacy:** By actively demonstrating commitment to inclusivity, government departments can build stronger relationships with all citizens, fostering greater trust and perceiving legitimacy in their actions.
  • Broader Reach and Impact:** Designing campaigns with accessibility in mind from the outset expands their potential audience, maximizing the impact of public service announcements and policy communications.
  • Compliance and Best Practice:** Adopting accessible event design standards, such as barrier-free paths and clear communication, aligns with international best practices and local regulations, mitigating potential legal challenges and enhancing reputation.

Source: Census and Statistics Department, The Government of the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region (2021). Statistics on persons with disabilities and chronic diseases in 2020.
