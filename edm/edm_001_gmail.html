SECTION 02 — 市場洞察 / MARKET INSIGHT
 
Phygital Civic Engagement / Hybrid Events Market Growth

Trend Watch: The "Phygital" Revolution Is Reshaping Gov Comms

Static exhibitions are yesterday's strategy. Here's what the data says about where engagement is heading.

The global hybrid events market was valued at USD 14.6 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow at a 12.8% CAGR through 2034 (MarketIntelo, 2026). Research in the Journal of Strategic Marketing (2026) confirms that "phygital" activations — blending physical installations with digital interactivity — consistently outperform traditional media in engagement metrics.

What this means for your next campaign:

→ QR-enabled installations and AR overlays increase dwell time 2–3x
→ Social-sharing mechanics extend reach beyond the venue
→ Digital engagement data gives measurable, auditable KPIs
→ Phygital formats serve youth to senior citizens alike

Source: MarketIntelo Hybrid Events Market Report 2026; Tandfonline Journal of Strategic Marketing 2026

GOVERNMENT Education Bureau — Curriculum Development Institute
 
GOVERNMENT & PUBLIC SECTOR

Beyond Information: How HK's Best Campaigns Turn Policy into Experience

When a Tram Becomes a Classroom: Reimagining Civic Education for the Public

From passive spectators to active participants — this is what modern public engagement looks like.

When the Education Bureau set out to transform their "A Voyage with Books Series" into a city-wide public experience, the brief was clear: make reading unmissable. Firebean answered with an immersive, multi-sensory exhibition at Times Square, Causeway Bay — anchored by a life-size Hong Kong tram installation and complemented by 3D-printed book sculptures and interactive digital panels.

The result was not an exhibition. It was a destination. By translating two abstract literary themes into tangible, walk-through environments, Firebean created a campaign that maximised foot traffic, deepened public engagement, and generated organic social media coverage.

Key Outcomes:

→ Immersive installation at a prime HK retail location
→ Multi-sensory design linking physical and digital touchpoints
→ Broad demographic reach across families, youth, and general public
