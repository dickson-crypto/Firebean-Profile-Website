Section 03 — Industry Insight
Smart City Tech in HK Gov 2025-2026
HK's Digital Leap: 110 Smart City Initiatives by 2025
Discover how Hong Kong's rapid smart city evolution reshapes government engagement and outreach.

Hong Kong is rapidly advancing its Smart City agenda, with the Digital Policy Office targeting 110 new digital government and smart city initiatives between 2024 and 2025. This significant push includes a focus on IoT, smart kiosks, and digital ID, aiming to enhance public services and citizen engagement. The "iAM Smart" platform, a cornerstone of digital identity, is projected to reach 17.5 million annual transactions by 2025, demonstrating a substantial shift towards digital interaction. Furthermore, the phasing out of old smart ID cards in 2025 underscores a commitment to modern, data-enabled infrastructure.

Implications for HK government departments:

  • Leverage new digital platforms and smart kiosks for targeted public information dissemination and service promotion.
  • Utilize data from IoT deployments and digital ID usage to personalize promotional campaigns and improve citizen outreach efficiency.
  • Integrate "iAM Smart" functionalities into campaign registration and feedback mechanisms to streamline citizen participation.
  • Develop campaigns that highlight the benefits of smart infrastructure, fostering public trust and adoption of new technologies.

Source: Digital Policy Office, HKSAR Government (2024-2025 Policy Addresses)
