Section 03 — Industry Insight
HK Electoral Participation & Civic Education
Boosting HK's Civic Pulse: The Power of Creative Engagement
Unpacking Hong Kong's voter trends and how innovative campaigns can re-energize civic participation.

Hong Kong's Legislative Council election in 2025 saw a voter turnout of 31.9%, a slight increase from 30.2% in 2021, yet still near historic lows since 1998, when turnout ranged between 44% and 58% (Reuters, 2025). This trend underscores a critical need for enhanced civic engagement and education. While specific civic literacy rates for Hong Kong are not widely published, global studies consistently link civic knowledge to increased political efficacy and participation (UNICEF Innocenti). Creative and accessible campaigns are vital to reverse declining participation by fostering a deeper understanding of civic responsibilities and the electoral process.

  • Re-evaluate communication strategies:** Shift from traditional announcements to interactive, digital-first campaigns that resonate with diverse demographics.
  • Invest in civic education initiatives:** Develop engaging programs that clarify electoral processes and the impact of participation, targeting both youth and adults.
  • Leverage community partnerships:** Collaborate with local organizations to co-create campaigns that address specific community needs and build trust.
  • Utilize data-driven insights:** Analyze voter behavior and demographic data to tailor messages and outreach efforts for maximum impact.

Source: Reuters, 2025.
