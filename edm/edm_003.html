Blooming Success: Engineering the 'Tulip Sea' for the HK Flower Show
Overcoming logistical challenges to deliver a breathtaking floral spectacle for LCSD.

For the Hong Kong Flower Show 2025, Firebean partnered with the Leisure and Cultural Services Department (LCSD) to create a vibrant 'Tulip Sea'. The challenge was immense: maintaining floral longevity in an exhibition setting requires precise drainage and upkeep. Firebean's team engineered an innovative, hidden drainage system beneath the aesthetic display, ensuring the tulips remained pristine throughout the event without compromising the visual impact. This project highlights our ability to merge creative vision with rigorous technical execution, delivering spectacular public amenities that stand the test of time.

Key Outcomes:

  • Innovative hidden drainage engineering
  • Maintained peak floral aesthetics for the entire show
  • Delivered a major public attraction for LCSD

Local Charm, Urban Reach: Elevating Regional Products in the City
Okinawa Arakaki Market's pop-up offers a blueprint for promoting local districts.

To introduce Ogi Village's Shikuwasa juice to urban consumers, Firebean designed a vibrant pop-up booth for Okinawa Arakaki Market. We focused on authentic theme design and visual identity to bridge the gap between a regional specialty and a fast-paced city audience, creating a memorable brand experience.

This approach is highly relevant for government departments tasked with promoting local districts, heritage sites, or regional initiatives. By employing commercial-grade experiential design, public sector campaigns can transform local features into compelling urban attractions, driving tourism, community pride, and active public participation.

Section 03 — Industry Insight
Experiential Marketing for Public Engagement
Engage, Recall, Act: Experiential Marketing's Edge
Live experiences drive deeper connections and superior ROI for public awareness.

Experiential marketing campaigns generate 2x higher brand recall than traditional advertising [1]. This trend highlights a significant shift in how audiences, including Hong Kong citizens, engage with public awareness initiatives. Unlike passive traditional ads, experiential campaigns immerse participants, fostering emotional connections and active participation. This leads to more memorable experiences, higher engagement, and a greater likelihood of message retention, offering superior value for public sector communication.

  • Enhanced Public Trust**: Direct, interactive experiences build stronger trust than one-way messaging.
  • Increased Message Retention**: Memorable events ensure key public messages resonate longer with citizens.
  • Cost-Effective Engagement**: Higher recall and word-of-mouth amplification reduce the need for repeated, costly traditional ad buys.
  • Data-Driven Insights**: Real-time interaction provides valuable feedback for refining future public campaigns.

Source: [1] Kande Photo Booths, "Experiential Marketing Statistics 2026"
