Section 03 — Industry Insight
HK Mental Health: Destigmatizing Through Creative Campaigns
Beyond Stigma: Creative Mental Health Campaigns for HK Gov
Uncover how innovative public sector communications can transform mental health perceptions in Hong Kong.

A recent study estimates that 46% of Hongkongers experience mild to severe symptoms of depression, anxiety, or both [1]. This significant prevalence underscores an urgent need for effective public sector communication strategies to destigmatize mental health and encourage help-seeking. While awareness campaigns can increase understanding, their true impact lies in fostering authentic, data-driven messages that resonate with the community.

  • Prioritize Empathy:** Develop campaigns that share relatable stories and experiences, moving beyond clinical terms to humanize mental health challenges.
  • Leverage Digital Platforms:** Utilize social media and interactive online channels to reach diverse demographics, especially younger generations, with engaging content.
  • Collaborate with NGOs:** Partner with mental health organizations to amplify messages and provide accessible resources, building trust and credibility.
  • Measure Impact:** Implement robust evaluation frameworks to assess campaign effectiveness in reducing stigma and increasing help-seeking behaviors.

Source: [1] MIND HK & Manulife Hong Kong (2025).
